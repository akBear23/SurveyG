INTRIGUING ABSTRACT:
==================================================
Unlocking the full potential of Large Language Models (LLMs) in specialized domains demands sophisticated knowledge integration beyond traditional Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG)'s limitations. This survey introduces **Graph Retrieval-Augmented Generation (GraphRAG)**, a transformative paradigm leveraging graph-structured knowledge to revolutionize LLM customization.

GraphRAG innovatively addresses RAG's shortcomings—from complex query understanding to context window constraints—by enabling superior hierarchical knowledge representation, efficient graph-based retrieval, and structure-aware knowledge integration. We systematically classify existing GraphRAG models into **Knowledge-based, Index-based, and Hybrid** frameworks, demonstrating their capacity for deep semantic nuance and multi-step reasoning often missed by conventional methods.

This comprehensive review provides a foundational analysis of GraphRAG's technical underpinnings, current implementations, and critical challenges. Offering an 'Awesome-GraphRAG' repository and practical guidance, it serves as an indispensable resource for researchers and practitioners. We delineate key research gaps and promising future directions, inviting the community to explore this pivotal field for advancing customized LLM applications.

3.  **Classification Framework**
    *   The survey categorizes existing GraphRAG models into three main types:
        *   **Knowledge-based GraphRAG:** Uses graphs as explicit knowledge carriers, transforming unstructured text into structured Knowledge Graphs (KGs) \cite{zhang2025}.
        *   **Index-based GraphRAG:** Utilizes graph structures primarily as indexing tools to organize and retrieve relevant raw texts efficiently \cite{zhang2025}.
        *   **Hybrid GraphRAG:** Combines the strengths of both knowledge-based and index-based frameworks for advanced reasoning tasks \cite{zhang2025}.

    *   It enables better representation of hierarchical relationships and complex knowledge dependencies, offering improved contextual reasoning and organized background knowledge for LLMs \cite{zhang2025}.
    *   Knowledge-based GraphRAG excels in explicit modeling of domain knowledge, while Index-based GraphRAG prioritizes efficient information retrieval and global navigation \cite{zhang2025}.
    *   The survey highlights GraphRAG's ability to capture deep semantic nuances and multi-step reasoning, which traditional RAG often misses \cite{zhang2025}.
