INTRIGUING ABSTRACT:
==================================================
The promise of intelligent computing systems hinges on robust, computable knowledge bases, yet populating ontologies with real-world, heterogeneous application data remains a formidable and often manual bottleneck. This paper introduces a novel, Model-Driven Engineering (MDE)-based generic metamodel designed to revolutionize **ontology population** by systematically extracting diverse data. Our innovation lies in a dual-objective design: achieving unprecedented **genericity** across various knowledge sources while ensuring its structure inherently mirrors that of an ontology. This architectural breakthrough provides a crucial intermediate representation, effectively bridging the gap between raw, unstructured data and formal **knowledge representation**. We demonstrate its instantiation for textual data extraction within the chemistry domain, offering a foundational component for automated, domain-independent knowledge base construction. By transforming a critical manual task into a structured, reusable process, this work significantly advances the state-of-the-art in **heterogeneous data extraction** and paves the way for more efficient development of **semantic web technologies** and truly intelligent systems.
