INTRIGUING ABSTRACT:
==================================================
Despite the widespread empirical success of Graph Neural Networks (GNNs), a profound theoretical gap persists in understanding their fundamental representational properties and inherent limitations. This paper rigorously addresses this critical void, presenting a novel theoretical framework to formally characterize the expressive power of GNNs.

We reveal a surprising truth: popular GNN variants, including Graph Convolutional Networks (GCNs) and GraphSAGE, possess inherent discriminative limitations, provably failing to distinguish even simple, non-isomorphic graph structures. To overcome these deficiencies, we introduce a new, remarkably simple GNN architecture. Crucially, we formally establish this proposed model as the most expressive within the class of neighborhood aggregation GNNs. Furthermore, we demonstrate that its discriminative power is equivalent to the Weisfeiler-Lehman (WL) graph isomorphism test, thereby establishing a theoretical upper bound for GNN expressiveness. These foundational results not only challenge existing assumptions about GNN capabilities but also provide a crucial blueprint for the principled design of truly powerful and discriminative graph neural networks, significantly advancing the theoretical understanding of the field.
