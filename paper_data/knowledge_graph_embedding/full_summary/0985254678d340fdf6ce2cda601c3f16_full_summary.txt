INTRIGUING ABSTRACT:
==================================================
Vision Foundation Models (VFMs) offer unprecedented generalization, yet their direct application to remote sensing images (RSIs) for critical tasks like change detection often falters. This performance gap stems from RSIs' unique characteristics, leading to VFMs' inadequate fine-grained feature extraction and imprecise boundary detection. We introduce **SFCD-Net**, a novel framework that ingeniously adapts the powerful Segment Anything Model (SAM) for robust remote sensing change detection. Our innovation lies in three synergistic components: a **parameter-efficient fine-tuning (PEFT)** method that imbues SAM with domain-specific knowledge, a novel **Bitemporal Feature Interaction (BFI)** module to significantly enhance change sensitivity, and a **Boundary Loss Function (BLF)** to refine boundary precision and small target recognition. Extensive experiments demonstrate SFCD-Net's superior adaptability and accuracy, outperforming existing models, particularly under limited computational resources. This work provides a crucial step towards unlocking the full potential of VFMs in specialized geospatial applications, offering a practical and powerful solution for accurate and resource-efficient change detection in RSIs.

*   **Research Problem & Motivation**
    *   Vision foundation models (VFMs) exhibit strong universality and generalization but perform poorly when directly applied to remote sensing images (RSIs) \cite{zhang2024}.

    *   The problem is important because effective change detection in RSIs is crucial for various applications, and challenging due to the domain shift and specific visual complexities (e.g., small targets, complex boundaries) \cite{zhang2024}.

*   **Related Work & Positioning**
    *   Existing approaches, primarily direct application of VFMs, suffer from limitations such as a lack of domain-specific knowledge, leading to insufficient fine-grained feature extraction and poor sensitivity to changes in RSIs \cite{zhang2024}.
    *   Previous solutions also struggle with accurately processing boundary details and recognizing small targets in RSIs \cite{zhang2024}.
    *   This work positions itself by proposing SFCD-Net, a novel network that integrates SAM with feature interaction, specifically designed to ameliorate these issues by adapting VFMs for RS change detection \cite{zhang2024}.
