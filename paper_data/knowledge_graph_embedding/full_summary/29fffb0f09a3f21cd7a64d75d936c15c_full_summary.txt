INTRIGUING ABSTRACT:
==================================================
Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) offer unparalleled potential for Click-Through Rate (CTR) prediction in billion-scale recommender systems, yet their direct application is severely hampered by overwhelming computational complexity and pervasive sampling bias. Existing GNN-based methods resort to aggressive neighbor sampling, sacrificing crucial behavioral information and compromising recommendation quality. We introduce a paradigm-shifting approach that fundamentally redefines the recommendation graph: the MAcro Recommendation Graph (MAG) and its tailored Macro Graph Neural Networks (MacGNN).

MAG intelligently groups individual "micro nodes" (users and items) with similar behavioral patterns into "macro nodes." This innovative graph structure drastically reduces the effective neighbor count from billions to hundreds, eliminating the need for biased sampling. MacGNN is a novel GNN specifically designed to efficiently aggregate information and revise embeddings on this macro-level graph. Extensive offline experiments demonstrate MacGNN's superior performance against twelve state-of-the-art CTR baselines while maintaining computational efficiency. Crucially, online A/B tests and successful deployment in Taobao's homepage feed, serving over one billion users, validate its real-world effectiveness and scalability. This work offers a principled solution to a long-standing challenge, paving the way for scalable and unbiased GNN applications in industrial-scale recommendation.
