INTRIGUING ABSTRACT:
==================================================
As the global population ages, fostering lifelong learning through digital platforms becomes paramount. However, **e-learning** environments are often not optimally designed for the unique **cognitive profiles** and **learning preferences** of **older adults**. This study addresses this critical gap by empirically investigating the attitudes and preferences of 103 Greek older adults (aged 55+) towards various **e-learning platform features** and **instructional design** elements.

Our findings reveal compelling insights: participants overwhelmingly favored **step-by-step content presentation**, the integration of **practice questions and examples**, and the inclusion of **assessment tests** after module completion. Crucially, the utilization of **explanatory videos** and **special graphics** was deemed imperative for effective learning. These data-driven insights are invaluable for developing **user-friendly** and highly effective **digital pedagogy** tailored specifically for **geriatric learners**. This research provides a foundational roadmap for enhancing **e-learning accessibility** and engagement, ultimately empowering older adults in the digital age and bridging the **digital divide**.
