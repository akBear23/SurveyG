INTRIGUING ABSTRACT:
==================================================
Multimodal Sentiment Analysis (MSA) is plagued by the persistent challenge of inter-modal heterogeneity, where distinct feature distributions hinder effective fusion. Existing model-level fusion methods often fall short, failing to adequately bridge these representation gaps. We introduce a groundbreaking **Multi-level Contrastive Learning and Multi-layer Convolution Fusion (MCL-MCF)** approach, redefining fusion as a progressive process across three distinct levels of semantic consistency. Our core innovation lies in applying **multi-level contrastive learning** to systematically alleviate heterogeneity: first between unimodal features, then between unimodal and already fused modalities, and finally at higher feature levels. This is synergistically integrated with a novel **Tensor Convolution Fusion (TCF) module** for extracting deep semantic features and a **Multi-layer Convolution Fusion (MCF) module** that models complex inter-modal relationships by fusing shallow and deep representations. Experiments on three public datasets demonstrate state-of-the-art performance, underscoring MCL-MCF's unparalleled ability to resolve multimodal heterogeneity. This work offers a transformative paradigm for MSA, paving the way for more sophisticated, staged fusion mechanisms in diverse multimodal learning tasks.
