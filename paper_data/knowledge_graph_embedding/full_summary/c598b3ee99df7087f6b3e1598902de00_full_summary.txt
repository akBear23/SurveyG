INTRIGUING ABSTRACT:
==================================================
Fibroblasts, ubiquitous stromal cells, exhibit profound heterogeneity, yet their diverse roles and distinct subtypes remain largely uncharacterized. Here, we present an unprecedented integrative single-cell atlas, meticulously classifying 249,156 fibroblasts from 10 diverse human tissues to decode their intricate landscape. Our high-resolution analysis reveals 18 distinct fibroblast subtypes, significantly expanding the known cellular taxonomy.

Most strikingly, we identify a novel, previously undescribed population: TSPAN8+ chromatin remodeling fibroblasts. These unique cells are characterized by high expression of genes associated with histone modification and chromatin remodeling, displaying elevated cell differentiation and resident fibroblast characteristics. Validated through spatial transcriptome and multiplexed immunofluorescence, TSPAN8+ fibroblasts predominantly interact with endothelial cells and T cells via VEGFA and F2R. Critically, their presence is significantly associated with poor prognosis across tissues. This comprehensive atlas not only redefines fibroblast biology but also uncovers a crucial, prognostically relevant cell population, offering profound implications for understanding tissue homeostasis, disease progression, and identifying novel therapeutic targets.
