INTRIGUING ABSTRACT:
==================================================
Unveiling subtle anomalies in complex time series data is critical yet challenging, often hampered by reconstruction-based methods that contaminate normal pattern learning and overlook vital frequency-domain information. We introduce FreCT (Frequency-augmented Convolutional Transformer), a novel unsupervised contrastive learning framework designed to overcome these limitations. FreCT innovatively models multivariate time series by integrating both time and frequency domains. It employs a hybrid encoder combining Transformer and convolution layers for comprehensive feature extraction, alongside a unique patch-based view generation. Crucially, FreCT leverages Discrete Fourier Transform for frequency-domain insights and introduces a robust contrastive loss using KL divergence with a stop-gradient strategy, making it resilient to anomaly contamination during training. Extensive experiments on four public datasets demonstrate FreCT's superior performance, consistently outperforming eleven state-of-the-art baselines in F1-score. This work significantly advances time series anomaly detection, offering a robust, dual-domain approach for more accurate and reliable system monitoring.

*   **Research Problem & Motivation**
    *   **Specific Technical Problem**: Time series anomaly detection, particularly addressing the limitations of existing reconstruction-based methods and the challenge of capturing complex sequential patterns.
    *   **Importance & Challenge**:
        *   Critical for system monitoring and risk identification across various domains (finance, healthcare, industrial).
        *   **Challenges**:
            *   Reconstruction-based techniques are susceptible to computational deviation from anomalies, leading to "impure" representations of normal patterns \cite{zhang2025}.
            *   Most methods primarily focus on time-domain dependencies, neglecting crucial frequency-domain information \cite{zhang2025}.
            *   Defining anomalies is difficult and often requires extensive expert knowledge.
            *   Anomalies are often triggered by complex, conjoint behavior of multiple variables.
            *   Deep learning methods often require labeled data, which is scarce for anomalies due to their rarity.

*   **Related Work & Positioning**
    *   **Relation to Existing Approaches**:
        *   Builds upon deep learning methods, particularly Transformer-based architectures and contrastive learning, which have shown promise in time series anomaly detection \cite{zhang2025}.
        *   Integrates Fourier transform, which has been empirically certified for time series analysis \cite{zhang2025}.
    *   **Limitations of Previous Solutions**:
        *   **Traditional methods (statistical, classic ML)**: Only capture shallow representations or dependencies, failing to model complex tendencies \cite{zhang2025}.
        *   **Deep learning methods (supervised/semi-supervised)**: Heavily rely on label information, which is problematic due to the rarity and disproportion of abnormal samples \cite{zhang2025}.
        *   **Most contrastive learning methods**:
            *   Often leverage reconstruction loss (e.g., MSE), which is easily magnified by abnormal segments, obstructing training and limiting the model's ability to learn pure normal patterns \cite{zhang2025}.
            *   Primarily focus on time-domain characteristics, ignoring valuable frequency-domain information that can reveal patterns not evident in the time domain \cite{zhang2025}.

*   **Technical Approach & Innovation**
    *   **Core Technical Method**: FreCT (Frequency-augmented Convolutional Transformer) is an unsupervised contrastive learning framework that models time series from both time and frequency domains.
    *   **Novelty**:
        *   **Contrastive View Generation**: Utilizes patch operations to generate two contrastive views (inter-patch and intra-patch levels) from the original data, avoiding the computational cost and potential deviation of traditional data augmentation \cite{zhang2025}.
        *   **Hybrid Encoder Architecture**: Employs a multi-layer encoder that integrates a Transformer module (for long-range dependencies) with a convolution module (for fine-grained local topology information) \cite{zhang2025}.
        *   **Frequency-Domain Augmentation**: Leverages Discrete Fourier Transform (DFT) to acquire frequency-domain representations of sequences, enhancing the model's ability to capture crucial characteristics beyond the time domain \cite{zhang2025}.
        *   **Robust Contrastive Loss**: Introduces a novel loss function combining:
            *   **Time-domain loss**: Kullback-Leibler (KL) divergence with a stop-gradient strategy to measure consistency, making the training process more robust against anomaly segments compared to MSE \cite{zhang2025}.
            *   **Frequency-domain loss**: Absolute error between the magnitude spectra of the two contrastive views to align frequency information \cite{zhang2025}.
        *   **Sequence-level Normalization**: Implements normalization across sequences rather than timestamps to deal with non-stationary issues and preserve interrelationships within sequences, avoiding over-smoothing \cite{zhang2025}.

*   **Key Technical Contributions**
    *   **Novel Framework**: Proposes FreCT, a novel framework for multivariate time series anomaly detection that measures consistency in both time and frequency domains \cite{zhang2025}.
    *   **Hybrid Encoder**: A patch-based Transformer integrated with convolution layers to efficiently capture both long-range dependencies and local features \cite{zhang2025}.
    *   **Robust Loss Function**: A contrastive loss based on KL divergence and a stop-gradient strategy, which reduces computational/spatial costs and overcomes the limitations of reconstruction loss by being less susceptible to anomaly contamination during training \cite{zhang2025}.
    *   **Frequency-Domain Integration**: Explicitly incorporates Fourier transformation to extract and align frequency characteristics, providing a more comprehensive understanding of time series patterns \cite{zhang2025}.

*   **Experimental Validation**

Ablation studies were conducted to validate the effectiveness of key components (frequency-domain analysis, KL divergence, stop-gradient).     *   **Datasets**: Evaluated on four public datasets: SWaT, WADI, SMD, and MSL \cite{zhang2025}.
    *   **Key Performance Metrics**: F1-score, Precision, and Recall \cite{zhang2025}.
    *   **Comparison Results**: FreCT consistently outperforms existing methods across all four datasets, demonstrating superior effectiveness and robustness in identifying anomalies, particularly in terms of F1-score \cite{zhang2025}. Ablation studies confirmed the significant positive impact of frequency-domain analysis, KL divergence, and the stop-gradient strategy on performance \cite{zhang2025}.
