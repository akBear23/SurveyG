INTRIGUING ABSTRACT:
==================================================
Unlocking the full computational potential of cultural heritage data remains a critical challenge. While Galleries, Libraries, Archives, and Museums (GLAM) embrace digital transformation, their innovative 'Labs' initiatives often publish rich datasets in traditional, less expressive formats, severely limiting machine-readability and advanced reuse for AI and Machine Learning applications.

Addressing this crucial gap, we present the first comprehensive, best-practice-driven framework for systematically transforming GLAM Labs metadata into **Linked Open Data (LOD)**. Our novel seven-step methodology, encompassing data modeling, **RDF** transformation, and semantic enrichment, provides a reproducible pathway for unlocking these valuable resources. We demonstrate its efficacy through a practical application to diverse datasets from the National Library of Scotland's Data Foundry, with all transformation processes documented via **Jupyter Notebooks**. This work not only provides tangible **LOD** datasets, accessible via **SPARQL** endpoints, but also offers vital guidelines for GLAM institutions worldwide. By fostering greater interoperability and discoverability, our approach empowers researchers to leverage cultural heritage for unprecedented insights, significantly advancing **Semantic Web** adoption and participation in the global **Linked Open Data Cloud**.

### Focused Summary for Literature Review: Towards a semantic approach in GLAM Labs

**1. Research Problem & Motivation**
*   **Specific Technical Problem:** GLAM (Galleries, Libraries, Archives, and Museums) organizations, particularly their "Labs" initiatives, publish digital collections in traditional, less expressive formats (e.g., MARC, Dublin Core, CSV). This limits their machine-readability, enrichment capabilities, and effective reuse by third-party actors and computational methods \cite{candela2023}. While many institutions adopt Semantic Web principles for their main catalogues, a structured approach for transforming datasets specifically from GLAM Labs into Linked Open Data (LOD) is lacking \cite{candela2023}.
*   **Importance and Challenge:**
    *   GLAM collections are increasingly vital for research, requiring computational access and datafication for Data Science, AI, and Machine Learning applications \cite{candela2023}.
    *   Adopting Semantic Web and LOD principles offers significant benefits: machine-readable, standard, and controlled vocabularies; enrichment through links to external repositories; and enhanced reuse \cite{candela2023}.
    *   This transformation is crucial for GLAM institutions to participate in international collaborative initiatives like the Linked Open Data Cloud and Wikidata \cite{candela2023}.
    *   The challenge lies in providing a systematic, best-practice-driven framework for this transformation, especially for the diverse and often experimental datasets found in GLAM Labs \cite{candela2023}.

**2. Related Work & Positioning**

**3. Technical Approach & Innovation**
*   **Core Technical Method/Algorithm:** The paper introduces a seven-step framework for transforming metadata datasets from GLAM Labs into LOD, emphasizing best practices \cite{candela2023}:
    1.  **Selecting a dataset:** Identification based on open licenses, documentation, permanent identifiers, data quality, and carbon footprint considerations.
    2.  **Data source analysis:** Preliminary exploration of original content (resource count, types, metadata fields, values) using tools like Pandas for CSV or XSLT/pymarc for XML.
    3.  **Extracting information:** Identification of key metadata elements (e.g., title, author) from source formats.
    4.  **Data modelling and transformation to RDF:** Adoption of standard controlled vocabularies (e.g., BIBFRAME, RDA), modeling data as RDF triples with URIs, and mapping original metadata to these vocabularies. This step can use software libraries (e.g., Apache Jena, RDFLib) or specialized transformation tools (e.g., marc2bibframe) \cite{candela2023}.
    5.  **Data enrichment:** Interconnecting resources with external repositories (e.g., Wikidata, VIAF) using `owl:sameAs` or specific linking properties.
    6.  **Data quality assessment:** Evaluation of the generated LOD using established criteria, often via SPARQL queries or Shape Expressions (ShEx).
    7.  **Publication:** Making the LOD datasets publicly available in standard formats (e.g., RDF/XML, Turtle, JSON-LD) with appropriate access methods (e.g., SPARQL endpoint, API) and documentation.
*   **Novelty/Difference:**
    *   The framework's specific focus on *datasets from GLAM Labs* addresses a previously unaddressed area in LOD transformation \cite{candela2023}.
    *   It provides a comprehensive, structured, and reproducible methodology, including practical considerations like data quality and environmental impact (carbon footprint) \cite{candela2023}.
    *   The application to datasets from the National Library of Scotland's Data Foundry represents a novel case study \cite{candela2023}.
