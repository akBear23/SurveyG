INTRIGUING ABSTRACT:
==================================================
Navigating the vast and complex landscape of biomedical information demands increasingly sophisticated AI. The twelfth BioASQ challenge (2024 edition) stands as a pivotal benchmark, pushing the frontiers of large-scale biomedical semantic indexing and Question Answering (QA). This paper offers a comprehensive overview of BioASQ 2024, detailing its four challenging shared tasks: from large-scale English QA and continuous iterative QA, to groundbreaking multilingual clinical entity detection (Spanish, English, Italian) and biomedical nested Named Entity Recognition (NER) in Russian and English.

We summarize the approaches and performance of 37 competing teams, who submitted over 700 systems. A striking trend is the widespread adoption of Large Language Models (LLMs) and Retrieval Augmented Generation (RAG), often synergistically combined with traditional NLP methods and transformer-based neural re-ranking. This integration, leveraging models like Llama-2/3, GPT-3.5/4, Mixtral, and Claude 3 Opus, demonstrates a significant paradigm shift. This overview provides crucial insights into the current state-of-the-art, highlighting continuous advancements and the growing imperative for robust multilingual and nuanced entity recognition capabilities in biomedical AI, making it essential reading for researchers shaping the future of health informatics.
