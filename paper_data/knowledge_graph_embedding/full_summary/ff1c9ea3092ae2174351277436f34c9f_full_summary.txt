INTRIGUING ABSTRACT:
==================================================
Unlocking the full potential of specialized training demands knowledge representation systems meticulously tailored to unique institutional contexts. This is particularly critical within the complex landscape of Mexican educational and military institutions, where generic approaches often fail to capture distinct teaching modalities and educational strategies vital for cognitive and formative development. This paper introduces a novel, context-specific process for constructing ontologies precisely designed for these environments. Our methodology meticulously integrates the unique structural, pedagogical, and strategic elements inherent to Mexican institutions, yielding sophisticated knowledge models. These custom-built ontologies serve as powerful training tools within the Military Education System, offering an unprecedented level of precision in representing and disseminating critical information. Preliminary evaluations demonstrate their acceptability and potential to significantly enhance user engagement and learning outcomes. By advancing the state-of-the-art in domain-specific ontology construction and evaluation, this work provides a robust framework for improving knowledge representation and fostering effective training across specialized educational contexts.
