INTRIGUING ABSTRACT:
==================================================
Unraveling the intricate relationship between brain structure and function is paramount for understanding cognition and neurological disorders. While structure-function coupling in the human cerebral cortex is well-established, the extent to which it is a universal group characteristic versus uniquely individual remains a critical, unresolved question. Here, we introduce a novel Graph Neural Network (GNN) framework to predict functional connectivity from structural connectivity, revealing a substantially stronger and more nuanced coupling than traditional correlation approaches. Our GNN-driven analysis demonstrates that this robust coupling is primarily driven by network topology. Crucially, we disentangle group-common effects, which dominate, from subtle yet significant individual-specific effects, uncovering a hierarchical organization along a sensorimotor-association axis. This groundbreaking work, validated across two independent datasets, provides an unprecedented understanding of structure-function dynamics, offering vital insights for personalized medicine, connectivity-guided therapeutics, and advancing our comprehension of individual differences in brain organization.
