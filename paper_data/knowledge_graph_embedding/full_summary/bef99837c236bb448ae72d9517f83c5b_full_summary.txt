INTRIGUING ABSTRACT:
==================================================
The relentless spread of fake news on social media poses a critical societal threat, yet accurate and early detection remains challenging, especially when crucial context like user comments is unavailable. We introduce **Multi-View Attention Networks (MVAN)**, a novel deep learning framework designed to tackle this problem by leveraging only source short-text tweets and their retweet propagation structures. MVAN innovatively combines a **Bi-directional Gated Recurrent Unit (BiGRU)** with attention for semantic text analysis and, for the first time in this domain, **Graph Attention Networks (GATs)** to encode complex news propagation patterns and identify suspicious users. This multi-attention architecture effectively fuses textual clues with structural insights, providing robust detection and crucial interpretability by highlighting key words and influential nodes. Evaluated on real-world datasets, MVAN significantly outperforms state-of-the-art methods, achieving an average accuracy improvement of 2.5%. Our work offers a practical, high-performing, and interpretable solution for **early fake news detection**, advancing the field by demonstrating effective veracity assessment under severe data constraints and paving the way for future graph-based social media forensics.
