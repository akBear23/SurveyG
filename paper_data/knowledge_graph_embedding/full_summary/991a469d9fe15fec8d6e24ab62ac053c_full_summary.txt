INTRIGUING ABSTRACT:
==================================================
Graph-based Machine Learning (ML) systems, increasingly deployed in high-stakes domains, often perpetuate insidious discrimination. Existing fair graph methods, whether statistical or counterfactual, critically overlook two pervasive sources of bias: hidden confounders influencing node features and graph structure, and inherent graph structure bias itself. This oversight leads to an oversimplified understanding of causality and an inability to generate truly authentic counterfactuals. We introduce the **Authentic Graph Counterfactual Generator (AGCG)**, a novel framework designed to revolutionize fair graph-based decision-making. AGCG uniquely mitigates graph structure bias through a **novel fair message-passing technique** and significantly improves counterfactual sample generation by **inferring hidden confounders**. Comprising modules for subgraph selection, fair node aggregation, hidden confounder identification, and counterfactual instance generation, AGCG offers a holistic, causally-aware approach. Our empirical studies on real and synthetic datasets demonstrate AGCG's effectiveness, advancing the state-of-the-art by providing a robust solution for more trustworthy and equitable graph ML systems.
