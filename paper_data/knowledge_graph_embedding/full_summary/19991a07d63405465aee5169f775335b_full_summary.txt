INTRIGUING ABSTRACT:
==================================================
Unraveling the enigmatic role of local lung immunity in SARS-CoV-2 infection, this study presents an unprecedented single-cell atlas of T cell responses within the human lower airway. Employing integrated single-cell RNA and T cell receptor sequencing (scRNA-seq, scTCR-seq) on bronchoalveolar lavage fluid (BALF) from 159 COVID-19 patients, we systematically characterized SARS-CoV-2-specific T cells throughout the disease course. We reveal that robust, highly activated, and metabolically distinct T cells are elicited in the BALF, irrespective of prior vaccination, exhibiting a potent tissue-resident memory (TRM) phenotype. Crucially, higher frequencies of these BALF-specific T cells correlate with accelerated viral clearance, reduced systemic inflammation, and improved respiratory function. While BALF CD8+ T cells were associated with positive outcomes, elevated specific CD4+ T cells surprisingly linked to severe disease markers. These findings underscore the critical, distinct protective role of mucosal T cells in controlling SARS-CoV-2, offering profound insights into COVID-19 pathogenesis and paving the way for targeted mucosal vaccine development strategies.

1.  **Research Questions & Hypotheses**
    This study investigates the phenotypic and functional profiles of SARS-CoV-2-specific T cells in the lower airway of COVID-19 patients throughout the disease course. It implicitly hypothesizes that these mucosal T cells are distinct from peripheral T cells, play a critical role in viral clearance and clinical outcomes, and that prior vaccination may have a limited impact on their induction.

2.  **Study Design & Methodology**
    The study employed an observational, integrated single-cell profiling design, combining molecular, transcriptomic, and T cell receptor (TCR) profiling at the single-cell level. Data collection involved intracellular cytokine staining (ICS), 29-color flow cytometry, single-cell RNA sequencing (scRNA-seq), and single-cell TCR sequencing (scTCR-seq) on bronchoalveolar lavage fluid (BALF) and peripheral blood mononuclear cells (PBMCs).

3.  **Data & Participants**
    The study included 159 COVID-19 patients (median age 64, 61.6% male) from January 2020 to January 2023, infected with wild-type, Delta, and BA.5 strains. A total of 122 BALF samples and 280 blood samples were analyzed, including 27 paired BALF and blood samples from 24 patients. The cohort comprised 55 vaccinated and 104 unvaccinated individuals, with 81 mild and 78 severe cases.

4.  **Key Empirical Findings**
    *   Robust SARS-CoV-2-specific T cells were elicited in BALF, irrespective of prior vaccination, and were distinct from those in peripheral blood.
    *   Higher frequencies of BALF-specific T cells correlated with diminished viral loads, lessened systemic inflammation, and improved respiratory function (e.g., higher PaO2:FiO2 ratio).
    *   BALF-specific T cells exhibited profound activation, proliferative capacity, multi-cytokine production (IFNÎ³, TNF), a glycolysis-driven metabolic signature, and a tissue-resident memory phenotype (TRM) after viral clearance.
    *   While BALF CD8+ T cells were associated with improved outcomes, higher specific CD4+ T cell levels in BALF were linked to markers of severe disease.

5.  **Statistical Analysis**
