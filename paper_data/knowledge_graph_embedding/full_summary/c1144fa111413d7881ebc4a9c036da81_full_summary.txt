INTRIGUING ABSTRACT:
==================================================
The advent of Industry 4.0, with its complex "learning factory" and "human-cyber-physical systems," necessitates a paradigm shift in knowledge representation. Traditional concept mapping falls short in creating the dynamic, semantic web-embedded knowledge bases crucial for both human and machine learning, failing to adequately address the intricate interplay of syntax, semantics, and pragmatics. This paper introduces **knowledge-aware concept mapping**, a novel approach designed to overcome these limitations. We propose a foundational framework integrating five distinct knowledge types—analytic a priori, synthetic a priori, synthetic a posteriori, meaningful, and skeptic—to guide the creation and analysis of robust concept maps. A real-life manufacturing case study illustrates the practical implications of these knowledge types. This innovative methodology provides essential rules and guidelines for developing semantically rich, context-aware knowledge systems, promising to significantly enhance discipline-based education and machine intelligence in smart manufacturing. This work is pivotal for researchers seeking to build the next generation of dynamic knowledge bases for Industry 4.0.
