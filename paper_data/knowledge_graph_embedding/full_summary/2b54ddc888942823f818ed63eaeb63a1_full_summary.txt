INTRIGUING ABSTRACT:
==================================================
Hyperspectral Image (HSI) classification demands models capable of capturing intricate global spectral-spatial dependencies, a challenge where traditional CNNs falter due to limited receptive fields and Transformers incur prohibitive quadratic computational costs. We introduce **3D-Spectral-Spatial Mamba (3DSS-Mamba)**, a pioneering framework that adapts State Space Models (SSMs) for efficient, long-range contextual modeling in high-dimensional 3D HSI data.

Our core innovation, the **3D-Spectral-Spatial Selective Scanning (3DSS) mechanism**, performs pixel-wise selective scanning along both spectral and spatial dimensions, generating flattened sequences processed by parallel S6 models. This novel approach effectively explores spectral reflectance and spatial regularity from a sequence modeling perspective, overcoming the limitations of causal sequences in traditional Mamba. By constructing five distinct scanning routes, we systematically investigate dimension prioritization. 3DSS-Mamba achieves superior performance over state-of-the-art methods on HSI classification benchmarks, demonstrating linear computational complexity while robustly capturing global spectral-spatial features. This work opens new avenues for applying efficient sequence models to complex multi-dimensional remote sensing data.

*   **Research Problem & Motivation**
    *   The paper addresses the challenge of Hyperspectral Image (HSI) classification, specifically the need for models that can effectively capture **global spectral-spatial contextual dependencies** while maintaining **computational efficiency**.

    *   Recent Mamba architectures, based on State Space Models (SSMs), offer linear computational efficiency and long-range sequence modeling capabilities but have primarily been explored in low-dimensional (1D language, 2D vision) scenarios, leaving their adaptability to high-dimensional 3D HSI data unexplored \cite{he2024}.

*   **Related Work & Positioning**
    *   **Traditional Methods:** Early HSI classification relied on hand-crafted features and subspace learning (e.g., SVM, LDA, EMP, EMAP), which are limited by their reliance on prior parameter settings and insufficient data fitting capabilities in complex environments \cite{he2024}.
    *   **Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs):** CNNs (e.g., 1D, 2D, 3D CNNs like SSRN) have shown strong performance in HSI classification due to local receptive fields and parameter sharing. However, they struggle to establish long-range dependencies, thus failing to capture global spectral and spatial characteristics effectively \cite{he2024}.
    *   **Transformers:** Transformers (e.g., ViT, HSI-BERT, SpectralFormer, SSTN, CASST) address the long-range dependency issue through self-attention mechanisms. Nevertheless, their inherent quadratic computational complexity O(N^2) poses significant challenges in terms of modeling efficiency and memory overhead, especially for high-dimensional HSI data \cite{he2024}.
    *   **State Space Models (SSMs) / Mamba:** Recent Mamba architectures (e.g., Mamba, Vim, VMamba) offer linear computational complexity and scalability for long-distance sequence modeling. While successful in 1D (NLP) and 2D (vision) tasks, their application to high-dimensional 3D HSI data remains largely unexplored \cite{he2024}.
    *   **Positioning:** This work positions itself as a novel approach that adapts the Mamba architecture to 3D HSI classification, aiming to combine the advantages of global spectral-spatial modeling (like Transformers) with linear computational efficiency (like Mamba) while overcoming the limitations of CNNs and Transformers \cite{he2024}.

*   **Technical Approach & Innovation**
    *   The proposed **3D-Spectral-Spatial Mamba (3DSS-Mamba)** framework is designed for efficient global spectral-spatial contextual modeling in HSI classification \cite{he2024}.
    *   **Spectral-Spatial Token Generation (SSTG) Module:** This module uses a 3D convolution block to convert the raw HSI cube into a set of 3D spectral-spatial tokens, preserving their inherent 3D structure \cite{he2024}.
    *   **3D-Spectral-Spatial Mamba Block (3DMB):** Multiple 3DMBs are stacked to extract comprehensive spectral-spatial semantic features. Each 3DMB integrates normalization, linear embedding layers, 3D convolution, and the core 3DSS mechanism \cite{he2024}.
    *   **Core Innovation: 3D-Spectral-Spatial Selective Scanning (3DSS) Mechanism:** This is the central technical innovation, specifically tailored to overcome the limitations of traditional Mamba (confined to causal sequences and low-dimensional data) for high-dimensional HSI \cite{he2024}.
        *   **3D-Spectral-Spatial Sequence Flattening:** It performs *pixel-wise sequential scanning* on 3D hyperspectral tokens along *both spectral and spatial dimensions*. This generates flattened 1D sequences, which are then processed bidirectionally (forward and reversed) \cite{he2024}.
        *   **Five Scanning Routes:** To investigate the impact of dimension prioritization, five distinct flattening routes are constructed: Spectral-priority, Spatial-priority, Cross spectral-spatial, Cross spatial-spectral, and Parallel spectral-spatial \cite{he2024}.
        *   **Selective Scanning with S6 Model:** The flattened sequences are fed into multiple parallel S6 (Selective State Space Model) instances, which perform dynamic, input-dependent selective scanning to model interactions among adjacent pixels. The outputs are then reshaped back into 3D structures and merged \cite{he2024}.
